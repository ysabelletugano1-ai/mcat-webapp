## Tutor Strategy

1. **Units first, always** — On Chem/Physics, check units before solving. Dimensional analysis eliminates 30% of wrong answers without any calculation.
2. **Estimate, don't calculate** — The MCAT rarely requires exact arithmetic. Round aggressively: 9.8 → 10, ln(2) → 0.7, e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹.
3. **Periodic table is your friend** — It's provided. Use it for atomic masses, electronegativity trends, and period/group comparisons.
4. **For electrochemistry: always identify cathode and anode first** — Reduction at cathode, oxidation at anode. Then E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode.
5. **Graphs encode the answer** — Chem/Physics passages almost always include a figure. The correct answer is usually the one consistent with what the graph shows, not what you expect theoretically.

## Common Traps

- **ΔG vs. ΔG°** — ΔG° is standard conditions; ΔG is real conditions. Equilibrium means ΔG = 0, not ΔG° = 0.
- **Confusing Km and Kd** — Km is Michaelis constant (enzyme kinetics); Kd is dissociation constant (binding). Both measure affinity but in different contexts.
- **Forgetting that pressure increases with depth** — P = P₀ + ρgh. Every 10 m of water = ~1 atm extra pressure.
- **Sign errors in electrochemistry** — E°cell must be positive for a spontaneous reaction. If it comes out negative, the reaction runs in reverse.
- **Le Chatelier's and catalysts** — A catalyst does NOT shift equilibrium position; it only changes the rate of reaching equilibrium.
