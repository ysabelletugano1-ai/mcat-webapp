# Transcription & Translation
**AAMC Category:** 1B | **Section:** Bio/Biochem | **Tier:** 1 | [[mcat-bio-biochem-hub]]

## Core Concept
The central dogma: DNA → RNA → Protein. Transcription copies one DNA strand into mRNA (nucleus in eukaryotes). Translation reads the mRNA codon by codon at the ribosome to build a polypeptide. Both processes use the template strand read 3'→5', producing product 5'→3'.

## High-Yield Facts
- **Template strand** (antisense): read 3'→5' by RNA polymerase; also called non-coding strand
- **Coding strand** (sense): same sequence as mRNA (but T instead of U)
- **Promoter**: upstream sequence where RNA pol binds; TATA box in eukaryotes
- **mRNA processing** (eukaryotes): 5' cap + 3' poly-A tail added; introns spliced out by spliceosome
- **Start codon**: AUG → methionine (always)
- **Stop codons**: UAA, UAG, UGA — no tRNA recognizes them; release factor binds
- **tRNA**: anticodon pairs with mRNA codon; amino acid attached at 3' CCA end
- **Ribosomes**: A site (incoming tRNA), P site (peptide chain), E site (exit)
- **Polyribosomes (polysomes)**: multiple ribosomes on one mRNA = efficient translation
- **Post-translational modification**: phosphorylation, glycosylation, ubiquitination, signal peptide cleavage

## Mnemonic
**Stop codons: "UAA, UAG, UGA = U Are Away, U Are Gone, U Go Away"**

## ⚡ Expert Shortcut
*Source: r/MCAT high-scorer posts*
When a question gives you a DNA sequence and asks for the protein: (1) Identify the template strand (usually told, or use the promoter), (2) Transcribe to mRNA (replace T with U, complement template), (3) Translate mRNA codons. Never try to directly translate from DNA — that's the #1 mistake.

## Common Trap
⚠️ The coding strand (sense strand) has the same sequence as the mRNA, but is NOT the template. The template (antisense) strand is read to make the mRNA. Students frequently use the wrong strand when given a question with both strands.

## ✅ Understanding Checkpoints

**Q1.** A DNA template strand reads 3'-TACGGATCC-5'. What is the corresponding mRNA sequence?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**5'-AUGCCUAGG-3'.** RNA pol reads the template 3'→5' and synthesizes mRNA 5'→3'. Complement each base and replace T with U: T→A, A→U, C→G, G→C, G→C, A→U, T→A, C→G, C→G. Result: AUGCCUAGG. Start codon AUG is present.
</details>

**Q2.** A eukaryotic pre-mRNA has 8 exons and 7 introns. After splicing, how many exons remain in the mature mRNA?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**8 exons.** Splicing removes introns and joins exons. All 8 exons remain in the mature mRNA. Introns are removed by the spliceosome. Alternative splicing can include or exclude specific exons, but the question specifies standard splicing.
</details>

**Q3.** During translation, a ribosome encounters the codon UGA. What happens?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**Translation terminates.** UGA is a stop codon. No tRNA has an anticodon complementary to UGA (UAA, UAG, UGA). Instead, a release factor protein binds the A site, the completed polypeptide is released, and the ribosome disassembles from the mRNA.
</details>

**Q4.** A drug blocks aminoacyl-tRNA from entering the A site of the ribosome. What is the direct consequence?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**Translation elongation is blocked.** The A site (aminoacyl site) accepts incoming charged tRNA. If no tRNA can enter, no new amino acids are added to the growing polypeptide. Initiation may occur normally (start codon recognized, first Met-tRNA binds P site), but elongation stops after the first peptide bond attempt. (Example: tetracycline antibiotics work this way in bacteria.)
</details>

**Q5.** How does the 5' cap on eukaryotic mRNA contribute to translation initiation?
<details><summary>Answer</summary>
**The 5' cap (7-methylguanosine) is recognized by eIF4E (initiation factor), which recruits the small ribosomal subunit to the 5' end of mRNA.** Without the cap, the ribosome cannot efficiently find the mRNA. The cap also protects mRNA from 5'→3' exonuclease degradation, increasing mRNA stability and thus the number of proteins produced per transcript.
</details>
