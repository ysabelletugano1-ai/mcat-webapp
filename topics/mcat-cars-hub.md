# Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills (CARS)

## At a Glance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Questions | 53 |
| Time | 90 minutes |
| Passages | 9 |
| Time per passage | ~10 minutes |
| Humanities | ~50% |
| Social Sciences | ~50% |
| Content to memorize | None |
| Score range | 118–132 |

**CARS is the only section with no content to memorize.** It is a pure skill — and skills only improve with daily practice.

---

## Reasoning Skills Tested

| Skill | What It Tests | % of Questions |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| Foundations of Comprehension | Main idea, author's thesis, literal meaning, specific examples | ~30% |
| Reasoning Within the Text | Inferences, integrating distant passage components, implicit assumptions | ~30% |
| Reasoning Beyond the Text | Applying passage ideas to new contexts, strengthening/weakening arguments, incorporating new information | ~40% |

---

## Tutor Strategy

1. **Never bring outside knowledge** — Every answer is defensible using only the passage. If you're using what you know about the topic, you're doing CARS wrong.
2. **Read the passage actively, not passively** — Identify: what is the author's main argument? What is the tone? What evidence do they use? Map this before reading questions.
3. **Eliminate, don't select** — Cross off the three wrong answers first. The correct answer is whatever survives elimination, even if it feels incomplete.
4. **"Most supported" ≠ "what I agree with"** — You may disagree with the author entirely and still be able to identify the answer most supported by the passage.
5. **For Reasoning Beyond questions: the passage is malleable** — The question may introduce a new fact and ask how it changes the argument. Treat it as a thought experiment, not a contradiction.
6. **Pace: 3 passages per 30 minutes** — Check your timer after passages 3, 6, and 9. If behind, skip an entire passage (guess and move on) rather than rushing through all of them.

---

## Common Traps

- **Too extreme language** — Answers with "always," "never," "all," "none" are almost always wrong on CARS.
- **Out of scope** — An answer may be true in the real world but not supported by the passage. Eliminate it.
- **Distorted paraphrase** — A choice that uses words from the passage but distorts the meaning is a trap. Paraphrase ≠ support.
- **Opposite polarity** — The answer that would weaken the argument when the question asks what strengthens it (and vice versa).
- **Reading too fast on the first passage** — Passage 1 sets the mental model for the whole section. Don't rush it.

## Practice Roadmap (8 Weeks — Daily)

1. **Weeks 1–5:** 1 Jack Westin CARS passage per day (free, high quality)
2. **Week 5:** Begin AAMC CARS section bank (official style)
3. **Weeks 6–8:** AAMC CARS only — do NOT practice with non-AAMC passages in the final stretch (third-party CARS trains different reasoning patterns)
4. **Post-FL review:** Spend 20 min per missed CARS question identifying which reasoning skill failed

## Passage Sources

**Use for practice (weeks 1–5):**
- Jack Westin free daily passages: jackwestin.com
- Blueprint CARS practice

**Use for final prep (weeks 5–8):**
- AAMC CARS Section Bank
- AAMC Full-Length CARS sections

**Do NOT use in final 3 weeks:**
- Princeton Review, Kaplan, or Altius CARS (different style; trains wrong instincts)
